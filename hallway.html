HALLWAY

The hallway is a place of wandering. It is a location between worlds, a road too narrow yet too open to be comfortable. Thus, this hallway is a representation of topics in displacement and alienation in Native American literature.

ABOUT THE AUTHOR

Mojave author Natalie Diaz has written two poetry collections and directed a Mojave language revitalization program. Before that, she was a professional basketball player who travelled to Europe and Asia ("Natalie"), which shows the diversity of indigenous experiences and values.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

Through her poem, Natalie Diaz is able to show the inevitable alienation that indigenous people face. If non-native Americans are unwilling to listen, there will never be understanding between the two parties, regardless of how indigenous people display their deep culture and traditions through artistic expression.

By telling a detailed story about the effects of government actions on an indigenous community, Natalie Diaz is able to show the buildup of tensions between the community and the federal government, and misunderstandings about indigenous communities.
