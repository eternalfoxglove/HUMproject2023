The gardens are at the back of the house. It is the last environment viewed before the end of the tour. We regroup in the gardens after individually exploring and understanding this house of knowledge.

Before we go, let's quickly discuss the difference in effects between stories about an individual and stories about a community.

Personal stories about an individual provide more specific examples, because everyone holds a slightly different experience. By acknowledging individual experiences, we avoid stereotyping and generalizations, and appreciate nuances.

Limitations

Like any study, this one has plenty of limitations. This study focuses specifically on the difficulties indigenous people face, but we should also research how indigenous authors express joy in their experiences or discuss their tribes’ golden ages. How do the more positive topics contribute significantly to the narrative, if at all? Additionally, the works covered in this study focus on authors born in the 1900s. Including more works by authors born in the 21st-century may lead to different conclusions.
